Letters of a Russian Traveler (1791-92)

    Major points
  • Letters of a Russian Traveler (1791-92), modeled after Laurence Sterne's "A Sentimenal Journey Through France and Italy (Karamzin published a translation of Sterne in his Moscow Journal in 1797-1801)
  • Blurred line between reality and textuality
  • Traveler essentializes cultures by thinking of them as unique entites instead of universal empires
  • Paris - fascination with coffee shops as a place where people discuss publicly new literary works
  • England - fascination with Brits who (while they know French) prefer to express themselves in their native English (Какая разница с нами!); post-Englightenment space where everyone has their own language
  • Patriotic longing for returning home (Я и в другой раз приехал бы с удовольствием в Англию но выеду из нее без сожаления)
  • Back home - "Грустить с моим сердцем и утешаться с друзьями" (sentimentalist cultural values)
