Archpriest Avvakum's Life, written by himself

    Features that are not common for hagiography
  • Use of profanity блядин сын, например
  • Avvakum writes in mixture of OCS and vernacular Russian of the 17th century, for example he write in colloquial language when he refers to his excommunication: "As to my excommunication...I wipe my arse with that" (427, cited in Zenkovsky).
  • Use of first-person narration
  • It is a travelogue, describing local experience (for example, the colonial practice of "amanat") (see Etkind, "Internal Colonization")
    Features of hagiography
  • Miracles happening in the life of a pious person. For example, "The ice gave a crack beneath me and split up to either side across the whole lake" (432). God helps Avvakum to get some water.
