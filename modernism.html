Modernism (1890s-1930s)

    Features of modernist literature
  1. Self-reflexive writing (skaz), for example Mikhail Zoschenko
  2. Fragmentation of experience, shift to short story cycles; poetry cycles
  3. Break with the past literary traditions (Futurist movement, for example Vladimir Mayakovsky and Velimir Khlebnikov); A Futurist Manifesto: A Slap in the Face of Public Taste (1912), inventing new language ("budetlyane")
  4. Cult of the author; the author can bring together the fragemented world (Vladimir Padunov)
    Representative texts
  • Petersburg (Andrey Bely, 1913)
  • Master and Margarita (Mikhail Bulgakov, 1928-1940, published in 1966 in USSR)
  • We (Evgeny Zamyatin, 1920, published in 1988 in USSR)
  • Aleksandr Blok
  • Isaac Babel
