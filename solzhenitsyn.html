Alexander Solzhenitsyn (1918-2008)

  • Religious writer/thinker
  • Village prose tradition: peasantry as paradise lost (another important writer is Valentin Rasputin)
  • Brings prisoner language into high literature (balanda, zek, zakosil kashu)
  • Pioneer in depicting informal economic practices under socialism (Ledenovo, Lipovetsky); i.e. farmers selling carpets
  • Taboo topics - gulag, cancer, POWs
  • Continues tradition of Russian realist novel
  • Anti-Soviet in content, socialist realist in form (argues Vladimir Padunov)
  • Blames intelligentsia for helping to establish Communist dictatorship (Tsezar)
