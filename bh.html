Bronze Horseman (1833, published in 1837)

    Major features
  • Hybrid genre, ode and dramatic narrative poem
  • Conflict between the emperor and common person (Evgeny)
  • Established the Petersburg tradition/myth in Russian literature
    Petersburg tradition
  • Ambiguity of experience (nature/culture, reality/dream, empire/individual)
  • The poem initiated the fantastic tale tradition in Russian literature (Todorov)
  • Other writers who inherited tradition: Gogol (Petersburg Tales), Dostoevsky (White Nights, The Double, Crime and Punishment), Andrey Bely (Petersburg), Bulgakov (Master and Margarita)
  • Doubling is a key device (locations, characters)
